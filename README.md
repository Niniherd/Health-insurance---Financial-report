1. Executive Summary

The network of branches (SEDES) has shown a trajectory of operational growth since 2023. However, the analysis of the projected closing for 2025 reveals critical liquidity concerns.

While accounting profitability (Net Income) remains within positive ranges for most branches, actual cash flow is experiencing significant pressure due to increasing operating costs and delays in accounts receivable collections.

3. Operational Efficiency and Accounts Receivable

Payment Cycle:
In October 2025, significant cash outflows are projected. For example, SEDE9 has scheduled supplier payments of -42,808.06 and accounts payable (costs) of -36,100.74.
It is imperative to negotiate payment terms with suppliers to prevent the collapse of the operating cash balance.

Accounts Receivable Risk:
The Allowance for Doubtful Accounts metric has shown significant fluctuations. By the end of 2025, branches such as SEDE4 and SEDE9 present high provision levels, indicating a deterioration in receivables quality that will impact future liquidity.

A negative value (such as -17,519.04 for SEDE4 or -40,695.18 for SEDE9 in October 2025) represents an increase in the estimate of uncollectible debts.

A positive value (such as the consistent 140.00 for SEDE1 during much of 2025) represents a favorable adjustment or recovery of previous provisions.

### Strategic Recommendations

Liquidity Shock Plan:
Immediately intervene in the management of SEDE5 and SEDE3 to reverse negative balances before the final closing of 2025.

Collections Optimization:
Strengthen the collections function in branches with the highest Allowance for Doubtful Accounts to convert outstanding invoices into available cash.

Liability Management:
Prioritize the repayment of interest-bearing obligations and renegotiate terms with suppliers in branches where the gap between Customer Collections and Supplier Payments is negative.
